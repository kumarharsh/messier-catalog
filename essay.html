Narrative Visualization Essay

Messaging

This interactive visualization tells the story of Charles Messier, and highlights his discoveries, and why his observations have become to be more famous than any of his discoveries.

Narrative Structure

This story follows the Martini Glass structure, where the readers are lead through the whole story, and at the end they are left to explore the data as they see fit. The first 2 charts offer just the visualization, but the last chart lets users interact with the chart and view each object in detail.

Visual Structure

The text-based story gives a narrative structure to the visualization. Each visualization is also connected by the common theme of Messier's lifetime and the years his observations and discoveries were made in. The catalog visualizations also have the year highlighted in bold so that the user has a sense of which data point relates to which year of discovery.

Scenes

There is only one overarching scene in this visualization - that is the story of Messier. It follows a chronological path through his life, and presents the relevant data when the readers have been informed about significant moments of his life.

Annotations

The first chart (comets) has annotations which highlight the period during which Messier recorded all the observations in his Catalog. The lines have the same colour as the object he recorded in his catalog (the Crab Nebula and M110).

Parameters

The visualizations work on two parameters

  1. which aspect is shown - the discovery phase, or the observation phase.
  2. the year of the discovery/observation.

The visualizations have these states:

  1. interactivity - once the reader has read through to the end of the article, the interactivity state is set to true, and after that, the reader can interact with any of the visualizations.
  2. publication year - Messier published two lists, and the last two visualizations show different list data.
  3. tooltip shown - once interactivity is enabled, the viewer can click on data points on the chart, and these actions fire of events which show tooltips with relevant data for each data point.

Triggers

The scroll is used to trigger off the interactivity and publication year, and the user click controls the tooltip shown state.

To guide the reader towards the content, animations are used when the reader has scrolled atleast 50% into a chart's area. The cursor also turns into a pointer to indicate to the reader that they can click the chart's data points for more data.
